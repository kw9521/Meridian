How Does the Stock Market Work?
The basics of buying and selling shares

When a company wants to raise money, it can sell small pieces of itself to the public. Each piece is called a share (or stock). When you buy a share, you own a tiny fraction of that company.

If the company does well and grows, your shares become more valuable. If it struggles, they lose value. You can sell your shares at any time on a stock exchange â€” like the NYSE or NASDAQ â€” which is essentially a giant marketplace where buyers and sellers are matched.

Stock prices change constantly during trading hours (9:30amâ€“4pm ET, Mondayâ€“Friday) based on supply and demand. If more people want to buy a stock than sell it, the price goes up. If more want to sell, the price goes down.
